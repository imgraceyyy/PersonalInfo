Introduction to the Internet

The Internet is a global network of computers that allows people to communicate, share information, and access digital services. It has changed how people live, work, study, and socialize. People can now send messages, watch videos, listen to music, attend online classes, and shop without leaving their homes.

The Internet is considered the most important technology of the Information Age. Just like electricity transformed the Industrial Age, the Internet transformed how people connect and access information. Today, billions of devices such as computers, smartphones, and servers are connected worldwide.

Uses and Importance of the Internet

The Internet is used in many ways in daily life:

People use it to search for information, read news, communicate through emails and video calls, send and receive files, and connect through social media. It helps people work faster, learn better, and stay connected even when far away. During times like lockdowns, the Internet became very important because people used it for online classes, work-from-home, and staying in touch with family and friends.

Internet Technologies: IoT and Cloud Computing

The Internet of Things (IoT) refers to devices connected to the Internet that can collect and share data. Examples include smart lights, air-conditioners, and security cameras. These devices can work automatically based on information they receive.


Advantages of IoT

  • Saves time and money
  • Allows devices to communicate
  • Provides automation
  • Makes life more convenient

Disadvantages of IoT

  • Higher risk of hacking
  • Data can be difficult to manage
  • Bugs can affect many devices
  • Compatibility problems

Cloud Computing allows people to store data and use applications through the Internet instead of on their own computers. Examples include Google Drive, Gmail, Microsoft 365, Dropbox, and Zoom. Cloud computing helps save money, allows easy access to files anywhere, supports teamwork, and provides better data security and backups.

Internet Security and Protocols

Internet security protects data and systems from online threats. It uses tools like encryption, firewalls, antivirus software, and secure websites. A safe website usually starts with HTTPS and shows a lock symbol.

To stay safe online:

  • Use strong passwords
  • Use two-factor authentication
  • Avoid clicking suspicious links
  • Use different email accounts for different purposes

Internet communication works because of Internet Protocols (IP). These are rules that control how data is sent.


Important protocols include:

  • TCP/IP - ensures data arrives correctly
  • HTTP - used for websites
  • FTP - used for file transfers

There are two versions of IP: IPv4 and IPv6, which help devices identify and connect with each other.
