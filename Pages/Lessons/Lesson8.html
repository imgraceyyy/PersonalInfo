Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)

Intellectual Property Rights protect creations of the mind. In the computing industry, this includes software, digital designs, databases, and online content. IPR ensures that creators have legal rights over their work and can control how it is used.


Why it's important:

  • Protects creativity and innovation
  • Encourages investment in new technology
  • Prevents unauthorized copying or misuse of digital products

Examples in computing:

  • Software code written by developers
  • Designs of mobile apps or websites
  • Digital music or videos

Copyrights

Copyrights give the creator of original work the exclusive right to reproduce, distribute, or display it. In computing, this mostly applies to software, videos, music, and online content.


Key points:

  • Copyright protects the expression of ideas, not the ideas themselves.
  • It prevents others from copying your work without permission.

Trademarks

Trademarks are symbols, logos, or names that identify a company or product. They protect a brand's identity so that consumers are not confused between similar products.


Key points:

  • Trademarks help businesses protect their reputation
  • They can be logos, slogans, or product names

Patents

Patents give inventors the exclusive right to use or sell an invention for a certain period of time. In computing, patents often cover new hardware, software processes, or algorithms.


Key points:

  • Patents encourage innovation by giving inventors recognition and profit
  • Patented inventions cannot be copied or sold without permission

Examples:

  • Software algorithms for encryption
  • Innovative computer hardware designs
  • Cloud computing processes

Ethics & Digital Counterfeiting

Ethics in computing involves using technology responsibly. This includes respecting copyrights, trademarks, patents, and avoiding digital counterfeiting.

Digital counterfeiting is illegally copying or distributing digital products like software, movies, music, or games.


Key points:

  • Using pirated software is illegal and unethical
  • Respecting digital rights ensures creators are rewarded for their work
  • Ethical computing includes honesty, fairness, and responsibility in all tech activities

Examples of ethical computing:

  • Using licensed software for school or work
  • Giving credit when using someone else's digital content
  • Reporting illegal downloads or counterfeit products
